# RAW.VIVES Product Context

## Register

brand

## Product

RAW.VIVES is the personal photographic archive of Alex Vicente. It presents a
curated body of photography and visual stories in Spanish and English while
preserving direct access to the archive, category filters, individual images,
licensing information, and contact.

The public identity is `raw.vives`, described as `Visual Archive` and authored
by Alex Vicente. `aleviclop.dev` is the related development identity, not a
replacement for the photographic brand.

## Users and purpose

- Visitors discovering Alex's photographic work on desktop or mobile.
- Editors, collaborators, and clients checking an image or requesting a license.
- Spanish- and English-speaking visitors who need equivalent navigation.
- Returning visitors who want direct, stable URLs to photographs.

The primary action is to explore the archive. Secondary actions are to understand
the authorial point of view, open an individual photograph, move between images,
and contact Alex without losing context.

## Brand personality

Nocturnal, tactile, and exact. The experience should feel like moving through a
photographic archive projected in a dark room: precise museum captions, generous
negative space, controlled contrast, and photographs that remain the brightest
and most expressive objects on the page.

The visual language combines cinematic photography, contemporary art direction,
editorial minimalism, and restrained digital experimentation. It is dark without
becoming gamer-like, technical without becoming clinical, and refined without
borrowing luxury-fashion clichés.

## Design principles

1. Photography leads; interface styling never competes with an image.
2. Editorial hierarchy comes from typography, scale, rhythm, and whitespace.
3. Technical metadata is quiet, legible, and consistently structured.
4. Bilingual navigation preserves route and content continuity.
5. Interaction remains semantic, keyboard-accessible, responsive, and resilient.
6. Motion is progressive enhancement and respects reduced-motion preferences.

## Anti-references

- Generic photography templates and conventional commercial galleries.
- SaaS card collections, dashboard surfaces, and excessive boxed layouts.
- Gamer neon, chromatic effects, decorative glow, or permanent glassmorphism.
- Luxury-fashion imitation based only on oversized serif typography.
- Dense magazine cosplay, ornamental clutter, and arbitrary gradients.
- Effects, cursors, or motion that compete with the photographs.

## Accessibility and quality

Target WCAG 2.2 AA contrast for interface text and controls. Preserve semantic
headings, visible focus, keyboard navigation, minimum 44 x 44 px touch targets,
200% browser zoom, reduced-motion support, image aspect ratios, and usable layouts
from 320 px through ultrawide screens. Visual-system changes must not materially
increase initial JavaScript or weaken the current static deployment model.
